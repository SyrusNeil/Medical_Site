Diabetes

Diabetes mellitus refers to a group of diseases that affect how your body uses blood sugar (glucose).

Glucose is vital to your health because it's an important source of energy for the cells that make up your muscles and tissues.

It's also your brain's main source of fuel.

Symptoms

1.Increased thirst

2.Frequent urination

3.Unexplained weight loss

4.Fatigue

5.Blurred vision

6.Extreme hunger

7.Frequent infections, such as gums or skin infections and vaginal infections

Risk Factors

Risk factors for Diabetes include:

1.Age. Your risk increases as you get older. This may be because you tend to exercise less, lose muscle mass and gain weight as you age.

2.Family history. Your risk increases if a parent or sibling has diabetes.

3.Weight. The more fatty tissue you have, the more resistant your cells become to insulin.

4.High blood pressure. Having blood pressure over 140/90 millimeters of mercury (mm Hg) is linked to an increased risk of diabetes

5.Abnormal cholesterol. If you have low levels of high-density lipoprotein (HDL), or "good," cholesterol, your risk of diabetes is higher.

Prevention

Prevention methods include:

1.Eat healthy foods.

2.Get more physical activity..

3.Lose excess pounds.
